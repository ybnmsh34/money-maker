What is a UUID?

A UUID (Universally Unique Identifier) is a 128-bit value used to identify information in computer systems. UUID version 4 is randomly generated and has a very low probability of collision.

UUIDs are commonly used in databases, distributed systems, and APIs to generate unique identifiers without coordination.
